# Carta de Poder: Sebastián de Lartaún to Cristóbal de Molina (1582)

## Document Description

This folder contains high-resolution images of the power of attorney 
(*carta de poder*) by which Bishop Sebastián de Lartaún of Cusco granted 
full legal authority to Cristóbal de Molina and Francisco Carrasco, 
secular priests resident in Cusco, to act on his behalf in all civil, 
criminal, and ecclesiastical matters before any court or judge in the 
Viceroyalty of Peru.

The document is dated September 24, 1582, and was executed before the 
royal notary Gómez de Baeza in Lima (City of the Kings), with witnesses 
Pero López, Pero de Góngora, and Pero Gonzales de Herrera.

## Significance

This document establishes that Cristóbal de Molina's relationship with 
Bishop Lartaún extended well beyond the composition of the *Account of 
the Fables and Rites of the Incas* (ca. 1575). Molina served as the 
bishop's legal agent with authority to act *in solidum* across all 
pending and future legal business in the viceroyalty. The date places 
the document within the period of the Third Council of Lima (1582–1583), 
which Lartaún attended as Bishop of Cusco.

The document is transcribed and translated in Appendix B of:

> Liliana Socorro Naranjo, *The Account of the Fables and Rites of the 
> Incas: Negotiated Translation, Indigenous Epistemologies, and Women's 
> Sacred Authority in Cristóbal de Molina's Relación* (Tuscaloosa: 
> University of Alabama Press, forthcoming).

## Note on the Date

The document is unambiguous: folio 3, lines 24–27 read *"veynte e quatro 
días del mes de setienbre de mill e quinientos e ochenta e dos años"* — 
September 24, **1582**.
